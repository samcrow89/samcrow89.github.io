About me

Hi!

I'm Bryan Search. I was born in Mainz, Germany in 1989 and grew up in a small town called Auerbach in der Oberpfalz as the son of German mother and a American father. I went to school in Eschenbach and got my Abitur(the highest German school graduation) in 2010.

After that I studied physics(B.S.) at the University of Bayreuth for a semester, until I realized that the lack of creative working in a scientific field couldn't satisfy the expectations I had in life. I had the chance to study at my university without a major subject for one semester and I used that to revue all the scientific fields that my university had to offer. I then soon found the faculty of media science and was amazed! I never thought of movies, games and TV-series as a possible working area before, and as soon as it struck my mind I knew that's what I want to do.

So in the Fall of 2011 I enrolled in Media Science and Media Practice(B.A.) with Applied Computer Science as my minor subject. There I started to work with video manipulation and soon get into visual effects. Later I switched my focus to game design and game development and got particularly interested in innovative game mechanics and their relation to level design. I also started programming my own games mostly in Unity, because it was free, powerful and easy to learn. I used it to test different game play concepts and learned a lot about remixing and adaption with experiments like building a board game version of the classic arcade game “Frogger” or making a 3D puzzle game based on sound design instead of graphics. Besides that, I got to know the toolchains and workflows of sound design, modelling and texturing. Even though I never got serious in these areas, I mention that because I think it's essential to know the workloads of your coworkers if you don't want to extend your deadline as a scrum master or producer.

At the moment I focus on AI programming. Therefore I wrote an A.I. for a stragety/shooter game in Unity that can handle up to 90 NPC and is freely expandable in its behavior because of the efficient way of decision making.

So much for now.

Bryan Search
